Tutorial

This tutorial was designed to give new users a quick introduction to the Fisheries Information Platform. The tutorial is arranged into five sections, each tackling the different components and tools available in the FIP. You can select the section you want to view although we recommend for you to work on the sections sequentially. It takes about 20 minutes to go through the entire tutorial.

We first discuss briefly the datasets used in the platform. The Section 1 of the tutorial focuses on the general configuration of the FIP, and then we dive in the details. Section 2 focuses on the main menu, and concepts such as adding layers, turning layers on and off and layer ordering. Section 3 explains how to search along the map. Section 4 discusses how to use the GIS toolbar to navigate the maps and extract point information from them and Section 5 provides additional, more specialized tools, such as the ability to save maps and generate reports for your data.

The FIP was developed using MapStore, an open source WebGIS framework developed to create, manage and share maps. If you are familiar with MapStore, you can surely skip most of this tutorial, just have a look at the Section 5 for information on some special tools that were created specifically for this project.
